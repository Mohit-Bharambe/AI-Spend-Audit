# Unit Economics & Value Proposition

This document breaks down the financial modeling behind SpendLens, demonstrating why offering a free AI spend audit is a highly profitable lead-generation channel for Credex.

## The Revenue Model

SpendLens is a free tool, but it functions as a highly qualified, top-of-funnel acquisition channel for Credex's primary business (SaaS procurement, credit management, and expense optimization). 

Instead of charging a flat software subscription fee for the audit, Credex captures value by taking a percentage of the recovered savings or facilitating the optimized procurement via their discounted credit marketplace.

## Realistic Math & Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)

Based on preliminary audit data, startups rapidly scaling their AI infrastructure frequently misallocate seats and subscribe to redundant, overlapping tiers.

### The Average Case
* **Average Startup Profile:** Seed/Series A with ~15 developers.
* **Identified Waste:** Unused seats on Cursor Business, redundant ChatGPT Team licenses overlapping with Claude Pro, and unoptimized OpenAI API leakage.
* **Annual Recoverable Savings:** ~$12,000 / year.

### The Credex Capture
If Credex facilitates the procurement optimization and takes a **15% performance fee** on the identified savings (or an equivalent spread on API credits):

* **Annual Customer Value (ACV):** $12,000 * 15% = **$1,800**
* **Expected Retention:** 3 years (as the startup scales and tooling needs evolve).
* **Customer Lifetime Value (LTV):** $1,800 * 3 = **$5,400**

## Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) vs. LTV

Traditionally, acquiring B2B SaaS procurement leads via paid search (Google Ads, LinkedIn) or outbound SDRs is incredibly expensive, with CAC often exceeding $1,500 to $2,500 per qualified lead.

By leveraging the **SpendLens Viral Loop**:
1. A founder runs a free audit.
2. The founder shares the high-shock-value public URL on Twitter or in a founder Slack channel.
3. Multiple peer founders click the link and run their own audits.

This organic, product-led referral mechanism drives the **Blended CAC** drastically down.

### Unit Economic Summary
* **LTV:** ~$5,400
* **Blended CAC:** < $50 (hosting, LLM inference, and minimal marketing ops)
* **LTV:CAC Ratio:** > 100:1

This makes SpendLens an incredibly asymmetrical bet. Even if only 1 in 10 generated audits converts to a paying Credex customer, the economic engine remains highly profitable due to the zero-marginal-cost nature of the automated software audit.
